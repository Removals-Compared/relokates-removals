How to choose a removal company: 10 things to check before you book

Published 6 May 2025  |  5 min read  |  By Relokates Removals

Choosing the wrong removal company is one of the most costly mistakes you can make on moving day. Damaged furniture, missing boxes, hidden charges added on the day and uninsured crews are all real risks when you book without doing proper due diligence. This guide gives you the 10 things every removal company should be able to confirm before you hand over your deposit.

The 10 checks every removal company must pass

1
Are they fully insured with goods-in-transit cover?

This is non-negotiable. Any professional removal company must carry comprehensive goods-in-transit insurance covering the full replacement value of your belongings, plus public liability insurance. Always ask to see proof — not just a verbal confirmation. If they cannot provide it, walk away.

2
Do they offer a fixed price or an hourly rate?

Hourly rates can spiral on moving day if access is difficult, lifts are slow or the move takes longer than expected. A fixed price means you know exactly what you are paying from the start. Always get a fixed price based on a proper survey of your home — not a guess over the phone.

3
Do they carry out a pre-move survey?

Any professional removal company should survey your home before quoting — either in person or by video call. A quote provided without a survey is a guess. Without seeing your belongings, a company cannot accurately price your move and may add charges on the day when the volume is larger than expected.

4
What do their reviews actually say?

Look for reviews on Google, Trustpilot or Which? Trusted Traders. Read the negative reviews as carefully as the positive ones — how the company responds to complaints tells you as much as the complaints themselves. A consistent pattern of 5-star reviews across multiple platforms is a strong indicator of reliability.

5
Are they a member of a trade association?

The British Association of Removers (BAR) is the leading trade body for removal companies in the UK. Members must meet minimum standards for insurance, equipment and professional conduct and are subject to a code of practice. Membership of BAR is a meaningful quality indicator.

6
What vehicles and equipment do they use?

A professional removal company should own and operate their own vehicles — not rely on hired vans. Ask about the size of vehicle they intend to use for your move and confirm it is appropriate for your volume of belongings. A crew arriving in a Transit van for a four-bedroom house is a red flag.

7
Are their staff employed or subcontracted?

Some removal companies subcontract moves to third parties, particularly at busy times. This means the people handling your belongings may never have worked for the company before and may not be covered by the company's insurance in the same way. Ask directly whether the crew on your move will be their own employees.

8
What is their cancellation and postponement policy?

House purchases fall through. Completion dates change. Your removal company should have a clear and fair policy for postponements and cancellations. Ask what notice period is required to change your date without losing your deposit and whether they have a waiting list policy for rescheduled moves.

9
Do they offer packing services and materials?

A full-service removal company should be able to pack your entire home if required, using professional-grade materials. Even if you plan to pack yourself, check whether they supply quality boxes and packing materials and whether these are included in the quote or charged separately.

10
What happens if something is damaged?

Ask directly — before you book — what the claims process is in the event of damage. A professional company will explain their process clearly, confirm the excess on their insurance and give you confidence that any claim would be handled promptly. Vague answers are a red flag.

Red flags to watch out for

Beyond the 10 checks above, there are several warning signs that should make you reconsider booking a removal company regardless of how competitive their price appears.

  • Demanding full payment upfront — A deposit is normal. Full payment before the move is not.
  • No physical address — A professional removal company should have a verifiable business address, not just a mobile number and email.
  • Very low quotes with no survey — If a company quotes significantly less than others without seeing your home, expect hidden charges on moving day.
  • No written confirmation — Everything should be confirmed in writing. A verbal quote or booking confirmed only by text is not sufficient.
  • Unwilling to provide insurance documentation — If a company hesitates when asked for proof of insurance, assume they do not have it.
