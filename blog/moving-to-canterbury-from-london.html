Moving to Canterbury from London: The Complete Guide

Published 1 Jul 2025  |  7 min read  |  By Relokates Removals

Canterbury is one of England's most historic and culturally significant cities, and an increasingly popular destination for London professionals seeking a genuinely different quality of life. The high-speed rail service from Canterbury West to London St Pancras takes just 56 minutes - transforming Canterbury's commuter proposition and opening the city to a new generation of London movers.

Commuting from Canterbury

The Southeastern Javelin high-speed service from Canterbury West to London St Pancras takes 56 minutes on the fastest trains. Services run throughout the day. Annual season tickets cost approximately £5,000 to £5,500. Many Canterbury residents commute two or three days per week and work remotely for the remainder.

Property in Canterbury

Canterbury offers outstanding value. A four-bedroom detached family home costs between £400,000 and £650,000. Properties within the city walls - period cottages, Georgian townhouses - command a premium but remain extraordinary value against London equivalents. Surrounding villages including Bridge, Wye and Littlebourne offer even more space for the budget.

Schools

The Langton Grammar School for Boys and Barton Court Grammar School for Girls are both highly regarded selective state schools. The King's School Canterbury is one of England's oldest and most prestigious independent schools. Canterbury Christ Church University and the University of Kent provide a significant academic community.

The Canterbury lifestyle

A UNESCO World Heritage Cathedral, the Marlowe Theatre, an outstanding independent food and drink scene, the Kent coast at Whitstable and Broadstairs within 20 minutes - Canterbury punches well above its weight for a city of its size. The quality of life here is genuinely distinct from anything available in or near London.

Canterbury's medieval street layout requires access planning

Many of the most desirable properties - within the city walls, along the river - sit on narrow medieval streets challenging for large vehicles. Relokates plans all Canterbury removals carefully with appropriately sized vehicles.
