Moving to Chelmsford from London: Everything You Need to Know

Published 20 May 2025  |  7 min read  |  By Relokates Removals

Chelmsford is consistently rated among the best places to live in England and it is easy to understand why. Essex's only city combines fast London connections with outstanding schools, a vibrant city centre, strong community identity and dramatically better value than equivalent London properties.

Commuting from Chelmsford

The fastest trains from Chelmsford to London Liverpool Street take as little as 33 minutes, running throughout the day at peak and off-peak. Liverpool Street connects to the Central line and the Elizabeth line. Annual season tickets cost approximately £4,500 to £5,000.

Property in Chelmsford

A budget of £500,000 that might buy a two-bedroom flat in Zone 3 London typically buys a four-bedroom detached house in a good Chelmsford suburb. Popular areas include Great Baddow and Galleywood to the south for family homes, Moulsham Street for young professionals and Broomfield and Springfield for newer build properties.

Schools

King Edward VI Grammar School and Chelmsford County High School for Girls are among the highest-performing selective schools in England. Several strong comprehensive schools and independent options including New Hall School in nearby Boreham complete the picture. Chelmsford's educational offer is one of the strongest in the South East.

Life in Chelmsford

A lively city centre with strong independent and high street retail, the Bond Street shopping area, excellent sports facilities and the County Cricket Ground home of Essex CCC. The River Chelmer provides a picturesque setting for walks and cycling throughout the city.

Moving day from London to Chelmsford

A typical London to Chelmsford move takes between four and seven hours. The A12 is the primary route from East London. Traffic during morning peak can be significant - we recommend early morning loads from the London property to arrive in Chelmsford before midday congestion builds.
