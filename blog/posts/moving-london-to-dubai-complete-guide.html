Moving from London to Dubai — The Complete 2025 Guide

Published 6 May 2025  |  12 minute read  |  By Relokates Removals

The London to Dubai relocation corridor is one of the most well-travelled in the world. Thousands of UK professionals, families and entrepreneurs make the move each year, drawn by the UAE's zero income tax, world-class infrastructure and outstanding career opportunities. If you are planning to move from London to Dubai in 2025, this guide covers everything you need to know — from visa requirements and customs rules to shipping options, costs and timelines.

Relokates specialises in international removals from the UK to Dubai and the UAE, covering over 165 UK departure points. We manage the entire process from export packing at your London property to delivery at your Dubai address.

Step 1 — Secure your UAE residence visa first

The single most important thing to do before planning your physical move is to secure your UAE residence visa. UAE Customs requires a valid residence visa before personal effects can clear customs as a duty-free import. Tourist visas are not sufficient. Without a residence visa, your belongings cannot clear customs and will be held in storage at your cost.

Your UAE employer will typically arrange your work permit and residence visa as part of your employment package. The process generally takes four to eight weeks and requires medical tests, biometric data and document attestation. Start this process as early as possible — ideally before you book your removal.

Important — visa timing

Your belongings must arrive in Dubai after your residence visa is issued, not before. Plan your shipping date to ensure the goods arrive at least two weeks after your visa is confirmed, to allow for any delays in the clearance process.

Step 2 — Choose between sea freight and air freight

The two main options for shipping your household goods from London to Dubai are sea freight and air freight. The right choice depends on your timeline, budget and the volume of items you are moving.

Sea freight

Sea freight is the most cost-effective option for a full household removal. Your belongings are loaded into a shipping container at your London property and transported by vessel from a UK port to Jebel Ali Port in Dubai. Transit time is approximately five to eight weeks door to door depending on the vessel schedule and routing.

For a full container (FCL), your belongings occupy the entire 20ft or 40ft container. For smaller volumes, a shared container (LCL) groups your goods with other shipments heading to Dubai, which reduces cost but may add slightly to the transit time.

Air freight

Air freight is significantly faster — typically five to ten days from collection to delivery in Dubai — but considerably more expensive, and practical only for smaller volumes. Many people moving from London to Dubai use a combination of both: sea freight for the bulk of their household goods and air freight for essential items they need immediately on arrival.

Step 3 — Understand UAE customs requirements

UAE Customs applies a strict set of rules to household goods imported as personal effects. When managed correctly by an experienced international removal company, the process is straightforward. The key requirements are as follows.

A valid UAE residence visa is required for duty-free import of personal effects. The visa must be valid at the time the goods clear customs.

A detailed packing inventory listing every item in the shipment must be prepared and submitted to UAE Customs. Relokates prepares this inventory as part of our service — every box, item and piece of furniture is individually listed.

Prohibited items include alcohol, pork products, narcotics, weapons, politically sensitive material and certain medications. We provide a full prohibited and restricted items list during your pre-move consultation.

The goods must be pre-owned. UAE Customs applies duty to new items. Personal effects that have been used prior to shipment are imported duty-free, but brand new items still in original packaging may attract import duty.

Step 4 — Decide what to bring and what to leave

One of the most important planning decisions for a London to Dubai move is what to ship and what to sell, donate or put into storage in the UK.

Dubai apartments and villas are often furnished to a high standard, and it may not be cost-effective to ship large, heavy furniture from London if equivalent or better items are available in Dubai. Electrical items also require consideration — the UAE uses 220-240V electricity with Type G sockets (the same as the UK), so most appliances are compatible, but older or specialist equipment should be checked.

Items worth shipping include personal belongings, clothing, books, artworks, sentimental items, children's toys and belongings, and quality furniture that you would struggle to replace affordably in Dubai. Items worth selling or leaving behind include large white goods, cheap flat-pack furniture and anything that would cost more to ship than to replace.

Consider UK storage for items you are unsure about

If you are uncertain whether you will want certain items in Dubai, Relokates offers UK storage solutions. Items can be stored at our facility and either shipped to Dubai later or returned to you in the UK if your plans change.

A London to Dubai removal requires more lead time than a domestic move. We recommend contacting Relokates at least eight to twelve weeks before your planned departure date. This allows sufficient time for the pre-move survey, quotation, visa processing, shipping booking and container scheduling.

Where do London professionals settle in Dubai?

The most popular areas for London professionals relocating to Dubai vary by lifestyle and professional background. Finance and legal professionals based in the DIFC tend to choose the DIFC area, Downtown Dubai or Business Bay for proximity to their offices. Families with children typically choose Jumeirah, Umm Suqeim or Arabian Ranches for their international school catchments and community feel. Those seeking a premium lifestyle with a waterfront address tend to choose Dubai Marina or Palm Jumeirah.

Financial considerations — the real numbers

The UAE's zero personal income tax is the most compelling financial argument for the London to Dubai move. A professional earning £100,000 in London after tax retains approximately £67,000. The same salary package in Dubai is received in full with no income deductions. Over five years, the financial difference is significant.

However, the cost of living in Dubai should be considered carefully. Accommodation — particularly in the premium areas favoured by London expats — can be expensive. Private health insurance is compulsory in Dubai and adds a meaningful cost. International school fees, if you have children, are a major expense. When planning your move, model your full cost of living in Dubai against your UK position before committing.

Frequently asked questions — London to Dubai removals

How long does it take to ship from London to Dubai?

Sea freight from London to Dubai takes approximately five to eight weeks door to door. Air freight takes five to ten days. Allow eight to twelve weeks from initial enquiry to delivery in Dubai to ensure all arrangements are properly in place.

Do I need my UAE visa before my belongings are shipped?

Your visa must be in place before the goods arrive and clear customs in Dubai. We advise planning the shipping date so that goods arrive at least two weeks after your visa is confirmed.

Can I ship my car from London to Dubai?

Yes, vehicle shipping from the UK to Dubai is possible. We can arrange this as part of your removal or independently. Transit time is approximately four to six weeks by vessel and additional UAE customs documentation is required for vehicle imports.

What happens if my Dubai property is not ready when my goods arrive?

We offer storage at both the UK and Dubai end if your new property is not ready to receive goods on arrival. Storage costs are quoted separately and we find the most cost-effective solution for your timeline.
