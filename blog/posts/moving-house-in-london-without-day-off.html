How to Move House in London Without Taking a Day Off Work

Published 6 May 2025  |  7 minute read  |  By Relokates Removals

Moving house is consistently rated as one of the most stressful life events - and for London professionals with demanding jobs, the prospect of taking one or more days off work to manage a move adds another layer of pressure. The good news is that with the right planning and a removal company that operates flexible hours, it is entirely possible to move house in London without using a single day of annual leave.

This guide is written specifically for busy professionals in London, Essex, Kent and West Sussex who want to move home without disrupting their working week.

Why most people think they need a weekday to move

The traditional model of house removals assumes a Monday to Friday working pattern - collect in the morning, deliver in the afternoon. This made sense when most removal companies operated standard business hours and when solicitors and estate agents also worked conventional hours.

In reality, the only part of a house move that is genuinely tied to business hours is the legal completion - the moment when the purchase or rental agreement is formally completed and keys are released. Everything else - packing, loading, transit and delivery - can be arranged around your schedule.

Understanding completion day and key release times

For a purchase, completion typically occurs when funds are transferred between solicitors - usually between 11am and 2pm on a weekday. Keys are then released by the estate agent. This part of the process does require someone to be available to collect keys during business hours, but it does not require you personally to be at the property with a removal van.

Many London professionals handle completion day by working from home, taking a half day rather than a full day, or arranging for a trusted person - a partner, family member or friend - to collect the keys and be present at the property while they continue working and join later in the day.

Ask your solicitor about afternoon completion

Request that your solicitor targets a morning completion so that keys are released by midday. This gives you a full afternoon for the physical move, which can often be completed within a few hours for a flat or smaller property.

Evening and weekend removals - what is actually available

Relokates operates seven days a week from 8am to 10pm, which means we can accommodate moves that start after the standard working day ends. An evening move - starting at 5pm or 6pm after you finish work - is a realistic option for flats and smaller properties where the volume of belongings can be moved in three to five hours.

Weekend removals are the most popular option for working professionals. Saturday moves in particular allow a full day without any work conflict. The only consideration is that weekends tend to book up faster than weekday slots - we recommend securing your weekend removal date at least six weeks in advance.

The pre-move packing strategy

The most time-consuming part of any move is packing. If you can complete the majority of packing during the evenings and weekends in the two to three weeks before your move date, the actual removal itself becomes significantly faster and less stressful on the day.

A practical approach is to work through one room per evening - starting with the rooms you use least and leaving your bedroom, kitchen and bathroom until the final few days. Use a consistent labelling system so you know exactly which box goes to which room in the new property.

Alternatively, Relokates offers a professional packing service where our team visits your property the day before the move and packs everything efficiently using professional-grade materials. This removes the packing burden entirely from your evenings and weekends.

The two-stage move - the professional's solution

One highly effective approach for London professionals is the two-stage move. Rather than moving everything in a single operation, you split the process across two sessions.

Stage 1 - Friday evening or Saturday: Move the majority of your belongings to the new property. Leave only the essentials you need for one or two more nights - a bed, bedding, bathroom items, work equipment and enough kitchen items to function.

Stage 2 - Sunday or the following weekend: Move the remaining items, clean the old property and return the keys.

This approach reduces the pressure on any single day, allows you to settle in properly at the new property before bringing everything else over, and avoids the chaos of trying to unpack an entire home in one evening.

Book a storage unit as a buffer

If your completion date and move-out date don't align perfectly, a short-term storage solution can bridge the gap. Relokates can collect your belongings from your old property and store them until your new home is ready - eliminating the need for a precise date match.

Key strategies for moving without time off work

  • Choose a weekend completion date - If you have flexibility on your completion date, request a Friday or Saturday. Some lenders and solicitors will accommodate weekend completions, which removes the weekday constraint entirely.
  • Use a full packing service - Let Relokates handle all the packing in the days before your move. This removes the single most time-consuming task from your personal to-do list.
  • Start packing early - Begin packing non-essential rooms four to six weeks before your move date, dedicating one to two hours per evening. By moving week, the majority of your home will already be packed.
  • Arrange key collection in advance - Confirm with your estate agent and solicitor what time keys will be released and who can collect them on your behalf if you cannot leave work.
  • Book an evening removal slot - If your new property is a flat or a small house within Greater London or close by, a 5pm or 6pm start is entirely feasible with our team.
  • Use professional unpacking - Relokates can unpack boxes at the new property and place items in the correct rooms, saving you hours of unpacking on evenings after work.

What about parking and access in London?

One frequently overlooked aspect of London house moves is parking. Removal vehicles require close access to both the loading and delivery addresses. In London, this often means arranging a parking suspension - a temporary suspension of parking restrictions on a public road to allow a removal vehicle to park.

Parking suspensions must be applied for from the local council, typically requiring five to ten working days notice and a fee of approximately £50 to £100 per day depending on the borough. Evening moves can actually be advantageous here, as parking restrictions in many London streets end at 6pm or 7pm, eliminating the need for a formal suspension.
