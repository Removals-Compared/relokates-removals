Moving from London to Kent: Everything You Need to Know

Published 6 May 2025  |  7 min read  |  By Relokates Removals

Kent's reputation as the Garden of England understates its appeal for London movers. A county of extraordinary variety - from the well-heeled commuter towns of the north and west, to the historic cathedral city of Canterbury, the dramatic White Cliffs coast and the rolling Weald countryside in between - Kent offers something for almost every lifestyle and budget.

Why London professionals choose Kent

The combination of fast rail links, dramatically better value for money and outstanding natural environment makes Kent consistently one of the most popular destinations for London relocators. A family leaving a three-bedroom semi in Zone 4 can typically afford a four or five-bedroom detached house in many parts of Kent for a similar budget. The quality of both state and independent schools in Kent is high - the county retains a grammar school system which attracts families from across London.

The best towns in Kent for London movers

Sevenoaks

One of Kent's most sought-after towns, Sevenoaks sits in the Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty of the North Downs and offers fast trains to London Charing Cross and Cannon Street in around 25 to 30 minutes. Property prices are among the highest in Kent but the combination of outstanding schools, beautiful countryside and fast London access justifies the premium. Popular with City and Canary Wharf professionals.

Tonbridge

A few miles south of Sevenoaks, Tonbridge offers a slightly more affordable entry point with trains to London Bridge and Charing Cross in around 40 minutes. The town has a good high street, excellent schools including Tonbridge School, and a strong community feel. Popular with families who want the Sevenoaks lifestyle at a more accessible price point.

Tunbridge Wells

Royal Tunbridge Wells is one of Kent's most complete towns - a vibrant independent retail scene, excellent restaurants, outstanding schools both state and independent, and fast trains to London Charing Cross in around 50 minutes. Property prices are high for Kent but well below equivalent London values. The Pantiles area is one of the most attractive addresses in the South East.

Maidstone

Kent's county town offers a different proposition - good value, central Kent location and a wide range of property types from Victorian terraces to modern new-build developments on the outskirts. Trains to London Victoria take around 70 minutes - longer than the west Kent commuter towns but significantly more affordable.

Canterbury

A world-class city in its own right, Canterbury is popular with those who are less dependent on daily London commuting - remote workers, retirees and those in local professional roles. Fast trains to London St Pancras take around 55 minutes on the high-speed service. Property prices are moderate by South East standards and the quality of life - cultural, culinary and academic - is outstanding.

Gravesend and Dartford

The most affordable part of Kent for those leaving London, north Kent towns like Gravesend and Dartford offer fast rail links to London Charing Cross and London Bridge in around 30 to 40 minutes. Popular with first-time buyers and young families making the move out of East London and South East London.

Kent's grammar school system

Kent is one of only a handful of English counties to retain a fully selective grammar school system. For families with children approaching secondary school age, this is a major draw - the grammar schools in Tonbridge, Tunbridge Wells, Maidstone, Chatham and Canterbury are among the highest-performing state schools in England. Competition for places is intense and preparation is important.

What to expect on moving day from London to Kent

A typical London to Kent move takes between four and nine hours depending on the volume of belongings, the specific London location and the Kent destination. Moves to west Kent towns like Sevenoaks and Tonbridge are often shorter due to their proximity to London. Moves to Canterbury, Folkestone or the coast involve a longer drive and should be planned accordingly.
