Moving from London to Basingstoke: A Complete Guide

Published 29 Jul 2025  |  6 min read  |  By Relokates Removals

Basingstoke is one of the best-kept secrets among London commuter towns. Fast trains to London Waterloo in as little as 45 minutes, dramatically affordable property by South East standards, good schools and easy access to both the Hampshire countryside and the M3 make Basingstoke a genuinely undervalued option for London movers.

Commuting from Basingstoke

The fastest trains to London Waterloo take 45 to 55 minutes with frequent services throughout the day. Annual season tickets cost approximately £4,000 to £4,500 - excellent value for the speed and quality of service. Basingstoke shares the South Western Railway line with Winchester, Fleet and Farnborough, giving residents multiple options along the corridor.

Property in Basingstoke

Outstanding value for a town with 45-minute London access. A four-bedroom detached family home costs between £350,000 and £500,000 - dramatically less than equivalent Surrey or West Sussex commuter towns with similar journey times. Significant new-build development provides good-quality, energy-efficient housing at accessible prices.

Best areas for London movers

Old Basing village

A historic and attractive village setting to the east of the town - the most sought-after area for those wanting character alongside practicality.

Kempshott and Hatch Warren

Southern suburbs of the town offering larger family homes, good primary schools and easy motorway access.

Chineham

Newer housing in the north of the town with easy access to the M3 and Basingstoke station. Popular with commuters prioritising ease of access.

What Basingstoke offers

The Anvil concert hall is one of the better regional venues in the South. Winchester - one of England's most beautiful cities - is 20 minutes to the south. The North Hampshire countryside, the Test Valley and the Hampshire Downs provide outstanding walking and cycling terrain within easy reach of the town.

Basingstoke is a large town - choose your neighbourhood carefully

The difference between the town centre and the surrounding villages and suburbs is significant in character and feel. Spend time researching which area suits your lifestyle before committing to a property.
