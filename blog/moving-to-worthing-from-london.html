Moving to Worthing from London: Everything You Need to Know

Published 15 Jul 2025  |  6 min read  |  By Relokates Removals

Worthing has undergone a quiet but significant transformation over the past decade. Once dismissed as a retirement town, it has attracted a younger demographic of Londoners seeking coastal living at genuinely affordable prices - and the town has responded with a growing independent food and drink scene, a creative community and meaningful investment in its cultural offer.

Commuting from Worthing

Fast trains from Worthing to London Victoria take between 65 and 80 minutes. Direct trains also run to London Bridge via East Croydon. Annual season tickets cost approximately £4,800 to £5,200. Many Worthing residents commute three days per week and work remotely for the remainder - the coastal commuter pattern that has become standard for this part of Sussex.

Property in Worthing

Worthing offers outstanding value for a coastal Sussex town. A four-bedroom detached family home costs between £450,000 and £700,000 - significantly less than equivalent properties in Brighton or Hove. The most desirable areas are west Worthing toward Goring-by-Sea, the Tarring village area and the seafront and clifftop properties with sea views at prices that would be unthinkable in equivalent London coastal locations.

The Worthing food and cultural scene

Montague Street and the seafront now have excellent independent cafes, restaurants and bars. The Connaught Theatre is a respected regional venue. The growing surf culture and farmers market have attracted younger residents and genuinely changed the demographic and energy of the town.

Beaches and outdoors

Seven miles of shingle beach with beach hut communities, a seafront promenade running almost the entire length of town and the South Downs National Park immediately accessible from the north - Cissbury Ring, one of England's largest Iron Age hill forts, overlooks the town from the Downs.
