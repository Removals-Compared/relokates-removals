Moving Tips

How to move house in London - the complete guide for 2026

📅 12 May 2026 🕐 7 min read 👥 Relokates Removals Team

Moving house in London can feel overwhelming, especially with the unique challenges the capital presents. From narrow Victorian streets to strict parking regulations, house removals in London require careful planning and local expertise. After helping thousands of families move across London, Essex, Kent and West Sussex, we've put together this practical moving guide for London 2026 to help make your relocation as smooth as possible.

Planning Your London House Move

The key to a successful house removal in London starts with early planning. London's property market moves fast, and removal companies get booked up quickly, especially during peak moving seasons from April to September. Start planning your move at least 8-12 weeks before your target date.

Setting Your Moving Timeline

Create a detailed timeline working backwards from your moving day. If you're moving within London or to areas like Essex, Kent or West Sussex, consider the following timeframes:

  • 8-12 weeks before: Book your removal company and start decluttering
  • 6-8 weeks before: Notify utilities, schools, and employers
  • 4-6 weeks before: Start collecting packing materials and confirm arrangements
  • 2-4 weeks before: Begin packing non-essential items
  • 1-2 weeks before: Pack everything except daily essentials
  • Moving week: Confirm all arrangements and pack remaining items

Choosing Your Moving Date Wisely

When you move house in London can significantly impact both cost and convenience. Avoid the last Friday of the month when possible, as this is traditionally the busiest moving day. Mid-week moves often offer better rates and more flexibility. Consider London's traffic patterns too - moving during school holidays can mean clearer roads but higher removal costs due to increased demand.

Understanding London Moving Regulations

London boroughs have specific regulations that can affect your house removal. Each area has different rules about parking permits, loading restrictions, and access times.

Parking Permits and Suspensions

Most London boroughs require parking bay suspensions for removal vehicles. Apply for these 5-10 working days in advance. Costs vary by borough, typically ranging from £15-£65 per bay per day. Popular areas like Camden, Westminster, and Kensington & Chelsea often require longer notice periods.

If you're moving to or from areas extending into Essex, Kent or West Sussex, check whether similar permits are needed. Many towns in these counties have adopted London-style parking restrictions in their town centres.

Congestion Charge and ULEZ Considerations

The Congestion Charge zone and Ultra Low Emission Zone (ULEZ) affect house removals in central London. Professional removal companies typically use ULEZ-compliant vehicles, but confirm this when booking. The ULEZ now covers areas within the North and South Circular roads, so factor this into your moving costs and timing.

Choosing the Right Removal Service for London

London house removals require specific expertise. Not all removal companies understand the unique challenges of moving in the capital or have experience with the diverse property types found across London, Essex, Kent and West Sussex.

Full Service House Removals

A full-service house removal includes packing, loading, transport, and unpacking. This option works well for busy professionals, families with young children, or anyone moving long distances from London to places like rural Kent or West Sussex. Professional packers know how to protect delicate items during London's stop-start traffic conditions.

Part Load and Shared Load Options

If you're moving a smaller property or don't need everything moved on the same day, part load services can reduce costs. This works particularly well for moves between London and surrounding areas in Essex, Kent or West Sussex, where routes are regularly serviced.

Specialist Services

Some London moves require specialist handling. Piano removals, antique furniture, or valuable art collections need expert care. Luxury removal services provide additional protection and white-glove handling for high-value items. If you're making an international move from London to places like Dubai, specialist international relocation services handle customs documentation and overseas shipping requirements.

Packing Strategies for London Moves

London properties often mean working with limited space, narrow staircases, and challenging access. Smart packing strategies can prevent damage and delays on moving day.

Room-by-Room Packing Approach

Pack one room at a time and label boxes clearly with both contents and destination room. This helps removal teams work efficiently, especially important when dealing with London traffic and time pressures. Use a colour-coding system for each room to make identification even quicker.

Special Considerations for London Properties

Victorian and Edwardian houses common across London, Essex, Kent and West Sussex often have narrow doorways and steep stairs. Pack heavier items in smaller boxes to make them easier to manoeuvre through tight spaces. Wrap furniture legs and protect corners, as these properties often have original features that can be easily damaged.

Essential Items Box

Pack a clearly marked box with essentials for your first night in your new home. Include phone chargers, basic toiletries, medications, tea and coffee, and a change of clothes. London moves can be unpredictable, and having essentials easily accessible reduces stress if unpacking takes longer than expected.

Managing Moving Day in London

Moving day in London requires coordination and flexibility. London's traffic, narrow streets, and parking challenges mean things don't always go exactly to plan.

Preparing Your Properties

At your old property, ensure clear access for the removal team. Remove any obstacles from pathways and protect floors and walls in common areas if you're in a shared building. Many London apartment blocks have specific moving hours, so confirm these in advance.

Have all boxes packed and ready before the team arrives. London removal crews work efficiently but delays in one property can affect the entire day's schedule.

Communication is Key

Stay in regular contact with your removal team leader throughout the day. London traffic can cause delays, so flexibility helps keep everything running smoothly. If you're moving between different areas like from central London to Kent or Essex, journey times can vary significantly depending on traffic conditions.

Final Checks

Before leaving your old property, check all rooms, cupboards, and storage areas one final time. Check lofts and cellars, common in older London properties. Take meter readings and photographs of the property's condition. If you're renting, this documentation can be important for getting your deposit back.

Settling Into Your New London Home

Once you've moved house in London, there are several immediate priorities to help you settle in quickly.

Immediate Essentials

Register with local services as soon as possible. Find your nearest GP surgery, dentist, and if you have children, confirm school arrangements. London areas can have significantly different facilities even within the same borough.

Set up your utilities and broadband. If you're moving to areas like rural Kent or West Sussex from central London, broadband options may be different from what you're used to, so research providers in advance.

Exploring Your New Area

Take time to explore your new neighbourhood. London areas each have their own character, and moving from one part of London to another can feel like moving to a different city entirely. Find local shops, transport links, and community facilities.

If you've moved from London to areas in Essex, Kent or West Sussex, you'll likely find a different pace of life and community feel. Local Facebook groups and community centres are great ways to meet neighbours and learn about local services.

Cost Planning for London House Removals

Moving costs in London vary significantly based on several factors. Understanding these helps you budget accurately and avoid unexpected expenses.

Factors Affecting Moving Costs

Distance plays a major role, but it's not just about miles. Moving from central London to nearby Essex might cost more than moving to West Sussex due to traffic congestion and access challenges. Property type matters too - moving from a ground floor flat is typically cheaper than a fourth floor Victorian conversion without a lift.

Time of year affects pricing, with summer months commanding premium rates. If you have flexibility in your moving date, consider moving in winter months for better rates and availability.

Getting Accurate Quotes

Always arrange for removal companies to survey your property in person or via video call. London properties can be deceptive, and accurate quotes depend on understanding your specific circumstances. Be honest about challenging access, the amount of belongings, and any special requirements.

Get quotes from multiple companies, but don't just choose the cheapest. Consider experience with London moves, insurance coverage, and reputation. A slightly higher quote from an experienced London removal company often provides better value than a bargain price from a company unfamiliar with London's challenges.

Storage Solutions During Your London Move

Many London moves require temporary storage, especially when downsizing or when there's a gap between selling and buying properties.

Short-term Storage Options

If you need storage for a few weeks during your move, look for facilities with flexible terms. Some removal companies offer integrated storage services, which can simplify logistics and reduce double handling of your belongings.

Long-term Storage Considerations

For longer-term storage needs, consider location carefully. Storage facilities in outer London or areas like Essex, Kent and West Sussex often offer better value than central London locations, but factor in access requirements and additional travel time.

Special Considerations for Different Types of London Moves

Moving to Suburban Areas

If you're moving from central London to suburban areas in Essex, Kent, or West Sussex, you'll likely have more space but different challenges. Garden furniture, lawnmowers, and outdoor equipment may need special handling. Check that your removal company has experience with these items.

International Moves from London

London is a major hub for international relocations. If you're moving overseas, perhaps to destinations like Dubai, you'll need specialist international removal services. These companies handle customs documentation, shipping arrangements, and can often provide destination services to help you settle in your new country.

Office and Business Relocations

If you're combining a house move with an office relocation, look for companies experienced in both residential and commercial moves. London's business districts have specific requirements and timing considerations that experienced removal companies understand.

Making Your London Move Stress-Free

While moving house in London will always involve some stress, proper planning and choosing the right removal company makes a significant difference. Focus on what you can control - early planning, clear communication, and realistic expectations.

Remember that London moves often take longer than expected due to traffic and access challenges. Build buffer time into your schedule and stay flexible when possible. Professional removal teams experienced with London conditions can handle most challenges that arise.
