Moving to East London: The Neighbourhood Guide

Published by Relokates Removals · 2026-08-12

East London has transformed more dramatically than almost any other part of the capital over the past fifteen years. Walthamstow, Hackney, Stratford, Leyton, Leytonstone, Bethnal Green, Bow - each has a distinct character and a very different price point. Here is the honest neighbourhood guide for anyone moving to East London.

Walthamstow E17

Walthamstow has become one of the most sought-after destinations for buyers moving east from Hackney or north from Stratford. The Village - a conservation area of Georgian and Victorian houses around the High Street - is the most desirable part. The William Morris Gallery, the wetlands at Walthamstow Reservoirs and one of the longest street markets in Europe are genuine assets. Victoria Line access from Walthamstow Central makes Central London genuinely accessible. Property prices have risen significantly but remain below equivalent Hackney stock.

Hackney E8, E9

Hackney is established rather than emerging now. London Fields, Victoria Park, Broadway Market - the infrastructure for a comfortable and sociable life is fully in place. It is expensive relative to the broader East London market. The best value is in the E9 postcode around Homerton, which remains slightly below the E8 premium.

Stratford E15

The Olympic legacy has been more uneven than the development plans suggested. Westfield is genuinely useful. The cultural facilities at the Olympic Park are excellent. The residential towers around the stadium vary significantly in quality and management. Stratford is good value for transport - Crossrail, Jubilee and Central lines plus Overground - but research individual buildings carefully before buying in the high-rise stock.

Leyton and Leytonstone E10, E11

The best value in inner East London right now. Victorian terraces with gardens at prices well below Walthamstow and dramatically below Hackney. Leyton Midland Road Overground provides a useful route. The high street has been improving but remains a work in progress. Early buyers in Leyton in 2010 to 2015 have seen significant capital growth - whether a similar trajectory repeats from 2026 onwards is the question buyers are asking.

Moving to East London - practical considerations

East London has extensive Controlled Parking Zones covering most residential streets. A removal van cannot park without a formal suspension from the relevant London borough. Relokates manages all parking suspension applications for East London moves as part of our standard service. Victorian terraces throughout E10, E11 and E17 often have narrow hallways and steep stairs - we carry specialist equipment for these properties as standard.
