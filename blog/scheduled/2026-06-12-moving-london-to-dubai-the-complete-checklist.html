Moving from London to Dubai: The Complete Checklist

Published by Relokates Removals · 2026-06-12

Moving from London to Dubai is one of the most complex relocations a household can undertake. The logistics, the customs requirements, the paperwork and the timeline all require careful planning well in advance. This is the complete checklist.

12 weeks before your move

Begin your UAE residency visa application if you have not already done so. UAE customs requires evidence of UAE residency to import household goods duty-free. Without a valid residency visa or Emirates ID, your shipment may be held at customs indefinitely.

Contact Relokates for a free international removal consultation. We will confirm the shipping timeline, advise on sea freight versus air freight, and begin the documentation process. Allow at least 12 weeks from first contact to delivery in Dubai for a sea freight shipment.

8 weeks before your move

Confirm your shipping method. Sea freight in a dedicated container is the most cost-effective option for full household moves - typically 5 to 9 weeks door to door. Air freight is 5 to 10 days but significantly more expensive per cubic metre. Most full household moves use sea freight.

Begin sorting prohibited items. UAE customs prohibits alcohol, pork products, narcotics, weapons, certain medications, gambling equipment and politically sensitive material. Begin removing these items from your household inventory now - do not leave this until packing day.

4 weeks before your move

Confirm your Dubai address and notify utilities. Set up a UAE bank account if you have not already - you will need this for deposit payments and utility connections at your new address.

Research international schools if you have children. The most sought-after Dubai international schools have waiting lists of one to two years. If you have not already secured school places, contact schools immediately.

Packing week

Our specialist export packing team will arrive with export-grade double-walled boxes and specialist materials. Every item is inventoried and assigned a declared value for customs documentation. This packing inventory is a UAE customs requirement - our team produces it as part of the packing process.

Electronics, jewellery and high-value items require individual declaration with purchase receipts or valuation certificates. Have these documents ready before packing begins.

After your shipment departs

Our UAE partner network manages customs clearance at the Dubai end. We keep you updated throughout and handle any customs queries directly. You will receive tracking information and an estimated delivery window once the shipment is at sea.

At your Dubai address

Our Dubai delivery team will place every item where you want it and remove all packing materials. We recommend a piano tuning appointment 2 to 4 weeks after delivery for any musical instruments, as they need time to acclimatise to the Dubai climate.
