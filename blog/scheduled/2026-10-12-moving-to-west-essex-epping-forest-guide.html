Moving to West Essex: The Epping Forest District Guide

Published by Relokates Removals · 2026-10-12

The Epping Forest district - Loughton, Chigwell, Theydon Bois, Buckhurst Hill, Epping itself - is one of the most consistently sought-after parts of Essex. Large houses, excellent schools, genuine countryside and a Central Line connection to London make it an enduringly popular destination for families leaving East London and the inner suburbs. Here is the honest guide.

Loughton IG10

Loughton is the most popular town in the district for London leavers. The High Road has good independent shops and restaurants. The Central Line from Loughton to Liverpool Street takes around 35 minutes. Debden is slightly cheaper and on the same line. The forest is genuinely on the doorstep - not a park, actual ancient woodland. Schools in Loughton and Debden are consistently rated good to outstanding. Property ranges from 1930s semis to substantial detached houses.

Chigwell IG7

Chigwell has a reputation built partly on a television programme and partly on genuine quality. The village itself is beautiful - Georgian houses around a green, a pub mentioned in Dickens and genuinely excellent schools including Chigwell School. Property is expensive by Essex standards. The Central Line from Chigwell station is slower than Loughton - factor in the journey time carefully.

Theydon Bois CM16

The most rural of the accessible forest villages. A green at its heart, a pub, a village shop and a Central Line station that takes you to Liverpool Street in 40 minutes. Property is genuinely expensive for what it is - you are paying for the setting and the quiet. Those who move here rarely leave.

Buckhurst Hill IG9

The best value in the district. Good Central Line access, a pleasant high street and proximity to the forest without the Chigwell or Theydon Bois premium. Popular with families who want the Epping Forest lifestyle at a more accessible price point.

Practical moving considerations

The Epping Forest district has a mix of property ages and types. Many of the most popular roads are in the established residential areas behind the high streets - narrow in parts with limited vehicle access. We assess access at both properties before every Essex move. The M11 and M25 provide good road access for the move itself - we always plan Epping Forest moves around the M25 junction 26 and 27 access points.
