How to Negotiate Your Removal Quote

Published by Relokates Removals · 2026-11-12

Most people accept the first removal quote they receive. Many accept the cheapest without understanding what they are actually comparing. Here is what you can legitimately negotiate on, what drives removal costs and how to get genuine value without compromising on quality or safety.

Understand what the quote actually includes

Before negotiating price, confirm exactly what is included. Does the quote include disassembly and reassembly of furniture? Packing materials? Parking suspension applications for London moves? Insurance? A quote that appears cheaper but excludes these services often costs more in total than a higher quote that includes everything.

What you can legitimately negotiate on

Date flexibility is the most effective negotiating tool. Midweek moves in the middle of the month are significantly less in demand than Friday end-of-month moves. If your completion date is flexible, ask your removal company whether a different day would come at a lower price. Many companies can offer meaningful flexibility on quieter days.

Volume reduction is the second most effective negotiator. Every cubic metre you do not move saves money. A serious declutter before your survey visit reduces the quoted volume and therefore the quoted price. Do not underestimate how much pre-move decluttering can reduce a removal cost.

What you should not try to negotiate on

Do not negotiate on insurance. Goods-in-transit insurance is not an optional extra - it is the protection that covers your belongings if something goes wrong. A company that removes insurance to offer a lower price is not giving you a deal.

Do not negotiate on crew size. If a removal company has assessed your property and quoted for a three-person crew, reducing to two people to save money will mean the job takes significantly longer - often making the total cost higher, not lower, if the company charges for additional hours.

Get three quotes and compare like for like

Three quotes from professional, insured removal companies gives you a genuine market rate. Compare them item by item, not headline price by headline price. The cheapest headline price almost always has exclusions that bring the real comparison closer to the others.
