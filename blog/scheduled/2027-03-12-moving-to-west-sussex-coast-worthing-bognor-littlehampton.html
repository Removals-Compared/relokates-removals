Moving to the West Sussex Coast: Worthing, Bognor and Littlehampton Compared

Published by Relokates Removals · 2027-03-12

The West Sussex coastal strip between Worthing and Bognor Regis attracts significant interest from buyers leaving London and the Surrey commuter belt. Three towns close together but with genuinely different characters. Here is the honest comparison.

Worthing

Worthing has had the most successful reinvention of the three towns. A creative and food scene that punches above its weight, a seafront that is genuinely enjoyable year-round, and a town centre with enough independent retail to sustain daily life. The train to London Victoria takes around 80 minutes on a fast service. Property prices are the highest of the three towns - Worthing is no longer the bargain it was five years ago but it represents reasonable value compared to equivalent coastal stock in East Sussex.

Bognor Regis

Bognor suffers from a reputation problem that its reality does not always deserve. The seafront has improved. The Butlin's resort provides year-round activity. Property prices are meaningfully lower than Worthing, which attracts buyers who want a coastal lifestyle but cannot stretch to the Worthing market. The train to London Victoria via Barnham is around 90 minutes. For buyers with realistic expectations and a limited budget, Bognor offers genuine value.

Littlehampton

Littlehampton sits between the two in price and is developing a character of its own. The harbour is attractive. The Arun estuary provides an unusual coastal landscape. The town centre is more limited than Worthing but improving. The train connection is via Barnham like Bognor - around 90 minutes to London.

Which one is right for you?

Worthing if you want the most developed town with the best food and social scene and can manage the price. Bognor if budget is the priority and you are comfortable with a less polished environment. Littlehampton if you are attracted to a harbour setting and want something slightly different from the typical coastal town.

Practical moving considerations

All three towns have streets close to the seafront with restricted vehicle access - narrow roads and limited parking near the most desirable properties. We assess access at both properties before every West Sussex coastal move and plan accordingly.
