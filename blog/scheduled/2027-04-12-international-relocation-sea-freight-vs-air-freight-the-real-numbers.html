International Relocation: Sea Freight vs Air Freight - The Real Numbers

Published by Relokates Removals · 2027-04-12

Sea or air freight for your international removal? The decision involves more than just speed. Here are the real cost comparisons, timelines and practical considerations for anyone relocating from the UK internationally.

Sea freight - the full picture

Sea freight is the standard choice for full household moves. A dedicated 20-foot container holds the contents of a typical two to three bedroom home. A 40-foot container accommodates a larger four to five bedroom property. Shared container options exist for smaller volumes - you share space with other shipments and pay only for the cubic meterage you use.

Door to door timeline for UK to Dubai via sea: 5 to 9 weeks depending on shipping line schedules, port congestion and customs clearance speed. Allow 10 to 12 weeks from first enquiry to delivery in Dubai to ensure all documentation, customs and shipping arrangements are properly in place.

Cost: A full 20-foot container from the UK to Dubai typically costs between £3,000 and £5,000 for the freight alone, plus packing, customs clearance and destination delivery. Total costs for a full service door-to-door move range from £6,000 to £12,000 depending on volume and services required.

Air freight - when it makes sense

Air freight is 5 to 10 days door to door. The cost premium is significant - typically three to five times the equivalent sea freight cost per cubic metre. For a full household move, air freight is rarely cost-effective.

Air freight works well for: essential items you need immediately on arrival while the sea shipment is in transit. High-value low-volume items - important documents, electronics, jewellery - where the cost premium is justified by urgency or value. Business moves where specific equipment is needed immediately.

The hybrid approach

Many of our international relocation clients use a combination. Air freight a small consignment of essentials and priority items. Sea freight the bulk of the household contents. This gives you functionality at the destination almost immediately while keeping the majority of the cost at sea freight rates.

What the cost comparison actually looks like

For a typical three-bedroom house moving from London to Dubai: sea freight full service approximately £7,000 to £10,000 total. Air freight equivalent volume would be approximately £20,000 to £30,000 for the same items. The hybrid approach - small air freight shipment plus sea freight for the main move - typically costs £9,000 to £13,000 total and gives you the best of both.
