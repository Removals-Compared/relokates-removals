Moving House With a Baby: The Complete Guide

Published by Relokates Removals · 2027-07-12

Moving with a baby or toddler adds a layer of complexity that most moving guides ignore entirely. Routine disruption, sleep regression, safety hazards on moving day and the challenge of settling into a new home with a small child who cannot understand what is happening. Here is the practical guide.

Timing around feeding and nap schedules

The removal van arriving at 8am is not compatible with a baby who feeds at 7 and naps at 9. Discuss timing with your removal company and be honest about your constraints. A slightly later start that works around your baby's morning routine is almost always possible and makes the entire day less stressful.

Childcare on moving day

If you can arrange for your baby or toddler to be with a grandparent, friend or childminder on moving day, do it. Moving day with a baby who needs attention is significantly harder than moving day without. Your focus needs to be on directing the removal crew, checking items off the inventory and managing the logistics of both properties. That is not compatible with a baby on your hip.

Pack the baby's essentials separately

The baby's essentials bag travels in your car, not on the van. Nappies, formula or expressed milk, a change of clothes, familiar toys, the sleep bag, the dummy, the baby monitor, medication. Everything the baby needs for 24 hours should be with you and accessible immediately on arrival at the new property - before the van is unloaded.

Set up the baby's room first

Before anything else is unpacked, set up the baby's sleeping space. Cot assembled, sleep bag accessible, familiar smell from home if possible. A baby who has a familiar sleeping environment settles more easily in a new property than one who faces a bare, unfamiliar room at bedtime on the first night.

Expect a settling period

Babies and toddlers notice environmental changes even when they cannot articulate them. Some sleep disruption and increased clinginess in the first week or two after a move is normal and will pass. Maintaining routines as consistently as possible during the transition helps.

Register with a new GP immediately

Do not delay GP registration after a move with a baby. Find your nearest surgery, check they are taking new patients and register on the first working day after your move. Babies need access to healthcare quickly when they need it - do not be in the position of not being registered when you need an appointment urgently.
