The Truth About Removal Company Reviews

Published by Relokates Removals · 2027-06-12

Google reviews are the primary way most people choose a removal company. They are also one of the most manipulated sources of information in the service industry. Here is how to read removal company reviews critically - what to look for, what the red flags look like and why 66 genuine reviews tells you more than 200 suspicious ones.

Volume versus authenticity

A removal company with 500 Google reviews and a 4.8 average sounds impressive. But 500 reviews accumulated by a relatively new company over a short period, all written in similar language, all from accounts with no other review history, is a significant red flag. Review buying is widespread in the removal industry. A company with 80 reviews accumulated over three years from detailed, varied accounts with genuine reviewer history is more credible than 500 quick five-star ratings with single-sentence text.

Read the negative reviews carefully

Every legitimate removal company will have some negative reviews. The question is how the company responded. A professional response that acknowledges the issue, explains what happened and describes what was done to resolve it demonstrates accountability. A defensive or aggressive response, or no response at all, tells you more about how the company will handle your problem if something goes wrong.

Check the reviewer profiles

Click on individual reviewers. A genuine review comes from an account with a profile photo, a review history covering multiple businesses over several years, and location information that makes sense. A fake review typically comes from an account created recently with no profile photo, no other reviews and a generic username.

Look for specificity

Genuine reviews mention specific details - the names of crew members, the specific items moved, the specific address or area, the time of year. Generic reviews that say excellent service and would recommend contain no information that could only come from genuine experience.

Trustpilot versus Google

Both platforms have fake review problems. Google is harder to manipulate than it used to be. Trustpilot has faced criticism for its business model allowing companies to manage their review display. For removal companies specifically, Google remains the more reliable platform.
