How to Move House During a Property Chain

Published by Relokates Removals · 2026-05-12

Property chains are the single biggest source of stress in the UK housing market. When your move depends on three or four other transactions all completing simultaneously, the risk of delay or collapse is significant. Here is how to protect yourself and get moved even when a chain is involved.

What is a property chain and why does it matter?

A property chain exists when your purchase depends on your buyer completing, and your seller's purchase depends on their buyer completing, and so on. A chain of four properties means four separate transactions, four sets of solicitors, four sets of buyers and sellers, and dozens of opportunities for something to go wrong. The longer the chain, the higher the risk.

How to strengthen your position in the chain

The strongest position in any chain is chain-free buyer or seller. If you have already sold and are renting, or if you are buying without a property to sell, you become the most attractive party in the chain. Estate agents and sellers will often accept a slightly lower offer from a chain-free buyer over a higher offer from someone with a complicated chain.

If you cannot be chain-free, the next best position is to have exchanged contracts on both your purchase and your sale simultaneously. Exchange creates legal obligation - neither party can pull out without financial penalty. Until exchange, anyone in the chain can walk away without consequence.

What to do if your chain collapses

Chain collapses are more common than buyers expect - industry estimates suggest around one in four property transactions in England falls through before completion. If your chain collapses, your options depend on where in the chain the break occurred.

If your buyer pulls out, you need a new buyer quickly. Instruct your estate agent immediately. If your seller pulls out, you need to decide whether to renegotiate or find another property. In both cases, your solicitor's fees to date are likely to be lost unless you have taken out home buyer protection insurance.

Home buyer protection insurance

For under £100 in most cases, home buyer protection insurance covers your legal and survey costs if the transaction falls through due to circumstances outside your control. For a transaction involving tens of thousands of pounds in costs, this insurance is worth taking out on every purchase.

Managing your removal booking during a chain

Booking a removal company when you are in a chain requires flexibility on both sides. At Relokates we work with clients in chains regularly. We recommend booking provisionally once you have a realistic completion window, and confirming the final date as soon as exchange happens. Most professional removal companies will hold a provisional date and confirm closer to the time.

The key rule is to never book and pay for a removal in full before exchange. Until exchange, the completion date is not legally binding and could change by weeks.

What happens to your belongings if completion is delayed on the day?

Day-of delays are common in chains. Funds do not clear on time, a solicitor is unavailable, a final search throws up an issue. If your belongings are on the van and completion is delayed, Relokates will hold your belongings securely and deliver as soon as the keys are released. Call us the moment your solicitor advises a delay - do not wait until the end of the day.
