How to Move House When You Are Self-Employed

Published by Relokates Removals · 2027-02-12

Self-employed buyers face challenges at every stage of the house buying process that employed buyers do not encounter. Mortgage applications, proof of income, address changes for clients and HMRC notifications all require careful management. Here is the complete guide.

The mortgage challenge

Most high street lenders require two to three years of self-employed accounts to approve a mortgage. If you have been self-employed for less than two years, your options are more limited but not non-existent. Specialist self-employed mortgage brokers have access to lenders who work with one year of accounts, SA302 tax calculations and accountant's references.

Your net profit figure - not your turnover - is what lenders assess for affordability. If you have been legitimately reducing your taxable income through expenses and pension contributions, this will affect the mortgage amount available to you. Discuss this with a broker before you start viewing properties.

Proving your income

Lenders typically require SA302 forms from HMRC for your last two to three tax years, plus the corresponding tax year overviews. Your accountant can also provide a letter confirming your income if required. Download your SA302 forms from your HMRC online account - do not wait for your accountant to request them as this adds time.

Address changes when you move

Self-employed people often use their home address for business purposes - Companies House registration, HMRC correspondence, business bank accounts, client invoices. Each of these needs to be updated when you move. Create a list of every organisation that holds your current address before moving day and work through it systematically in the two weeks after your move.

HMRC specifically

Update your address with HMRC through your personal tax account online. If you operate through a limited company, update your registered address at Companies House - this can be done online and takes effect immediately. Do not leave HMRC address updates - correspondence sent to your old address will not be forwarded by Royal Mail indefinitely.

Taking time off around the move

Self-employed people often feel unable to take time off around moving day. The reality is that trying to manage clients and the physical realities of a house move simultaneously rarely works well. Plan for reduced capacity in the week of the move and communicate this to clients in advance.
