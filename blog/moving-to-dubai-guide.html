Moving from the UK to Dubai: A Complete Relocation Guide

Published 6 May 2025  |  7 min read  |  By Relokates Removals

The decision to move from the UK to Dubai is one of the most significant a professional or family can make. The rewards - zero income tax, world-class infrastructure, outstanding career opportunities and an exceptional lifestyle - are well documented. But the process of physically moving your household from the UK to Dubai requires careful planning. This guide covers the essentials for British families and professionals planning the move.

Understanding the UAE residence visa

Before planning any aspect of your physical move, you must understand the UAE residence visa process. Without a valid UAE residence visa, your personal effects cannot clear UAE Customs as a duty-free import. Your employer in Dubai will typically arrange your employment visa and residence permit as part of your employment package. The process involves medical tests, biometric data and document attestation and typically takes four to eight weeks. Start this process as early as possible.

Sea freight vs air freight

The two main shipping options from the UK to Dubai are sea freight and air freight. Sea freight takes five to eight weeks door to door and is cost-effective for full household volumes - the cost of a 20ft container from the UK to Dubai is typically between £2,500 and £4,500 depending on origin and specific requirements. Air freight takes five to ten days but costs significantly more per cubic metre and is practical only for smaller volumes or time-critical items.

Most families moving from the UK to Dubai use a combination of both - air freight for essentials needed in the first few weeks and sea freight for the bulk of their household goods.

What to ship and what to leave behind

One of the most important planning decisions is what to bring to Dubai and what to sell, store or donate in the UK. Dubai has excellent furniture retailers and the cost of shipping large, heavy or low-value items may exceed their replacement cost in Dubai.

Worth shipping: personal belongings, quality furniture, artworks, children's toys and belongings, clothing, books, sentimental items, electronics.

Consider selling or leaving: large white goods, cheap flat-pack furniture, garden furniture, anything that would cost more to ship than to replace in Dubai.

UAE customs - what you need to know

UAE Customs applies a structured set of rules to household goods imported as personal effects. The key requirements are a valid UAE residence visa, a detailed packing inventory of every item in the shipment, and confirmation that goods are pre-owned personal effects rather than new commercial items. Prohibited items include alcohol, pork products, narcotics, weapons and certain medications. Where British expats settle in Dubai

The most popular areas for British families relocating to Dubai vary by lifestyle. Families with children typically choose Jumeirah, Umm Suqeim or Arabian Ranches for their community feel and international school catchments. Finance professionals tend to choose the DIFC area or Downtown Dubai. Those seeking waterfront living choose Dubai Marina or the Palm Jumeirah. Each area has a strong British expat community and good access to British curriculum international schools.

The financial case for the Dubai move

The UAE's zero personal income tax is the most compelling financial argument for British professionals. The same gross salary represents significantly more take-home pay in Dubai than in the UK. A professional earning £100,000 gross retains approximately £67,000 after UK tax and National Insurance. In Dubai, the same package is received in full. Over five years in Dubai, the cumulative tax saving for a senior professional can be substantial.
