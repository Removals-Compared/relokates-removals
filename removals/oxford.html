Property in Oxford

Oxford's property market is highly competitive with prices among the highest in England outside London. Jericho and Summertown are the most sought-after residential areas with Georgian and Victorian terraces commanding significant premiums. East Oxford around Cowley Road has more affordable Victorian housing popular with academics and young professionals. Headington and Marston have larger family houses. New-build developments at Barton Park and other sites add to the stock.

Getting to and from Oxford

Oxford station has direct services to London Paddington in approximately 55 to 70 minutes and Chiltern Railways services to London Marylebone. The coach network provides frequent services to London Victoria via the M40. The A34 and M40 provide fast road access. Oxford is approximately 60 miles from central London.

About Oxford

The Bodleian Library, one of the oldest in Europe and a legal deposit library, is Oxford's most remarkable institution. The Ashmolean Museum, the world's first university museum, houses Egyptian mummies to modern art. Covered Market, operating since 1774, is a unique Oxford institution. Christ Church, New College and Merton Quadrangle are among the most beautiful enclosed spaces in England.

Oxford's economy is driven by the University of Oxford, Oxford University Hospitals NHS Trust, Oxford Brookes University and a significant biomedical and technology cluster. BMW manufactures the MINI at the Cowley plant. Publishing is another major employer with Oxford University Press headquartered in the city.

Oxford has outstanding state schools including Cherwell School and Oxford School. The independent sector includes the Dragon School and Magdalen College School. University college places create additional demand for family housing near good schools.

Moving checklist for Oxford

When planning your move to or from Oxford, complete these important tasks well ahead of moving day to avoid last-minute complications:

  • Check the A34, A40 and M40 for planned works on your moving day
  • Book parking suspensions well in advance for Oxford city centre properties
  • Plan around university term times if moving within or near Oxford city
  • Contact Oxfordshire County Council for address changes and council tax
  • Research local schools and GP surgeries well ahead of your move date
