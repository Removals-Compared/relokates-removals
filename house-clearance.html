A house clearance service built on respect and responsibility

A house clearance is often required at a difficult time. The death of a loved one, a move into care, a family home being sold after decades, a relationship ending — the circumstances that lead to a house clearance are frequently emotional and demanding. At Relokates, we approach every house clearance with the sensitivity these situations deserve. Our team is respectful, discreet and professional throughout, and we take the time to work at a pace that suits you rather than rushing through someone's home and history.

Beyond the emotional dimension, house clearances also carry an environmental and practical responsibility. Filling a skip with everything and sending it to landfill is neither the only option nor, in most cases, the best one. We take a responsible approach to every clearance, separating items that can be donated, sold or recycled from those that genuinely need to be disposed of. Where possible, we work with local charities and organisations to ensure that usable furniture, clothing, books and household items find a second home rather than a landfill.

When house clearance is needed

  • Bereavement clearances: Clearing a property after the death of a family member is one of the most emotionally demanding tasks a family faces. We carry out bereavement clearances with particular care and sensitivity, working at your pace and ensuring that valuable or sentimental items are identified and preserved before the main clearance takes place.
  • Downsizing: Moving from a large family home to a smaller property means leaving behind furniture, belongings and decades of accumulated items. A house clearance carried out alongside a removal allows you to take what you need to your new home and have the rest managed responsibly on the same day.
  • Property preparation for sale: An empty, clean property photographs better, shows better and sells faster than one full of furniture and personal belongings. We carry out clearances for estate agents, property developers and private sellers across London, Essex, Kent and West Sussex.
  • Care home moves: When a family member moves into a care home, their property often needs to be cleared relatively quickly. We work with families in this situation regularly and understand both the practical and emotional dimensions involved.
  • Rental property clearances: Landlords dealing with a property left in a poor state by outgoing tenants, or clearing a property between tenancies, can use our clearance service to bring the property back to a lettable condition efficiently.

Our clearance process

Before we begin any clearance, we carry out a walk-through of the property with you or your representative to identify any items that should be kept, items of potential value that should be assessed before disposal, and any specific requirements you have for particular rooms or categories of items.

Once the walk-through is complete and you are satisfied that we have a clear picture of what should happen to each category of items, we begin the clearance systematically. We separate items into those being kept and returned to you, those suitable for donation, those suitable for recycling and those requiring disposal. We then remove all items from the property, leaving it clean, empty and ready for its next purpose.

We always aim to leave properties in broom-clean condition after a clearance. While we do not provide deep cleaning services ourselves, we can recommend trusted local cleaning companies across our coverage areas if a more thorough clean is required.

Responsible disposal and recycling

We are committed to minimising the volume of clearance items that go to landfill. Furniture in good condition is offered to local charities and organisations. Clothing, books and smaller household items are sorted for donation where appropriate. Electrical items are disposed of through approved WEEE recycling channels. Metal, cardboard and other recyclable materials are separated and recycled appropriately. Only items that genuinely cannot be reused, donated or recycled are disposed of as waste.

We are licensed waste carriers and all waste disposal is carried out in compliance with UK waste regulations. We can provide waste transfer notes and documentation if required for commercial or estate purposes.

House clearance costs depend on the volume of items to be removed, the size of the property, the access conditions and the distance to disposal or recycling facilities. We provide a free no-obligation quote following a brief discussion about the property. In some cases — particularly where there are significant volumes of saleable or recyclable items — we may be able to offset some of the clearance cost against the value of recovered items.
For urgent clearances — such as those required for probate or property sale deadlines — we can often accommodate bookings within a few days. For standard bookings, we ask for at least one week's notice to ensure we can allocate the right team and vehicles. We will always try to accommodate your timeframe.
Our team is experienced in identifying items that may have value beyond their apparent utility — antique furniture, vintage clothing, collectibles, jewellery, silverware and similar. If we identify items that we believe may be of significant value, we will bring these to your attention before proceeding with disposal. For formal valuations, we can recommend local antiques dealers and estate agents who specialise in property contents valuations.
