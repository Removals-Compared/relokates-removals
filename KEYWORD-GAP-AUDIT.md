## The gap: 5 keyword clusters you don't compete for

Each cluster below is a real search pattern with real commercial
intent. For each I've estimated rough UK monthly search volume
(based on typical removals industry data) and ranked by impact.

### Gap 1 — Service × Town pages (HIGHEST IMPACT)

Right now every service intent for any town funnels to the same
generic `/removals/[town]` page. Competitors win these queries
because they have dedicated pages:

**What customers search:**
- `man and van [town]` — high volume, lower-end commercial intent
- `office removals [town]` — high commercial intent, B2B
- `packing services [town]`
- `storage [town]` (or `[town] storage`)
- `house clearance [town]`
- `furniture removals [town]`
- `student removals [town]` (in uni towns)

**Why you don't rank:** you have no `/man-and-van-rochester` page.
Your `/removals/rochester` mentions the service in passing but
doesn't target the specific service-keyword combination.

**Recommended build:** Top 30 highest-value towns × 5 services
= **~150 new pages**. NOT all 496 towns × all services (3,968
thin pages = Google demotion). Pick by likely commercial value:
all London boroughs + Maidstone, Canterbury, Sevenoaks, Tunbridge
Wells, Chelmsford, Basildon, Colchester, Southend, Grays, Brentwood,
Chichester, Crawley, Horsham, Worthing, Bognor Regis.

Each page = 600-800 words of genuinely unique town+service content
(not boilerplate). URL pattern: `/man-and-van/[town]`, `/office-removals/[town]`.

### Gap 2 — Corridor pages (HIGH IMPACT)

Customers planning a move search `removals [from] to [to]` — 
specifically a corridor, not a town. Your blog has some of these
as informational guides but no commercial-intent landing pages.

**What customers search:**
- `removals london to brighton`
- `removals london to manchester`
- `removals essex to kent`
- `chelmsford to london removals`
- `house removals london to surrey`
- `man and van london to brighton`

**Why this matters:** corridor searchers are typically 2-4 weeks
from booking — peak commercial intent.

**Recommended build:** ~30 corridor pages covering the top routes
based on your existing GSC impression data and obvious corridors:
London → Kent, London → Essex, London → West Sussex, London →
Surrey, London → Brighton, Essex → London, Kent → London, plus
inter-county routes (Chelmsford → Colchester, Maidstone → Canterbury,
etc.).

URL pattern: `/removals/[from]-to-[to]` (e.g. `/removals/london-to-brighton`).
Each = 500-700 words. Reuse content from existing blog corridor
guides but make it a commercial-intent landing page (with quote
CTA above the fold, not buried under a guide).

### Gap 3 — Cost / pricing keywords (HIGH VOLUME)

These have the highest absolute search volume in the removals
category. You have one blog post that touches on costs, but no
dedicated landing page targeting these queries.

**What customers search:**
- `how much does a house removal cost` — very high volume
- `removal costs london`
- `man and van prices [town]`
- `removal company cost`
- `2026 removal price guide` (or current year)
- `2 bedroom removal cost`
- `3 bedroom removal cost`

**Why this matters:** these are top-of-funnel and informational
but they're how high-intent customers start their journey. Rank
here and you capture leads weeks before they're ready to book.

**Recommended build:** 5 pages.
- `/house-removal-costs` (UK-wide pillar)
- `/removal-costs-london` (London-specific)
- `/removal-costs-kent` (Kent-specific)
- `/man-and-van-prices` (service-specific)
- `/office-relocation-costs`

Each = 1,500-2,000 words. Include actual price tables (1-bed,
2-bed, 3-bed, 4-bed averages with ranges). Real numbers = trust.

### Gap 4 — Intent-qualified urgency keywords (MEDIUM IMPACT, HIGH CONVERSION)

Last-minute searchers convert at 3-5x the rate of planners.

**What customers search:**
- `same day removals [town]`
- `last minute removals [town]`
- `urgent removals london`
- `weekend removals [town]`
- `cheap removals [town]` — high volume, weaker margin
- `affordable removals [town]`
- `removals near me` — handled via local SEO + GBP, not a page

**Why this matters:** urgent intent = booking today. Even position 4
with a snippet that says "Same day removals available" can outconvert
position 1 from a generic page.

**Recommended build:** 3-5 pages.
- `/same-day-removals` (UK-wide)
- `/last-minute-removals`
- `/weekend-removals`
- Could also add `/cheap-removals-london` but only if you're
  genuinely competitive on price — otherwise you waste the rank.

### Gap 5 — Comparison and "best" keywords (MEDIUM IMPACT, BRAND DEFENCE)

When customers are short-listing, they search:
- `best removal companies london`
- `top removals in [town]`
- `relokates vs anyvan` (or other competitors)
- `anyvan alternatives` (you should be capturing these)
- `cheapest removals london` (only if competitive on price)

**Why this matters:** these capture late-funnel decision makers
who are already convinced they need a removal company — they're
just picking who. Lose this and you lose deals.

**Recommended build:** 5-10 pages.
- `/best-removals-london`
- `/best-removals-essex`, `/best-removals-kent`, etc.
- `/relokates-vs-anyvan` (and other top competitors)
- `/anyvan-alternatives` (purely defensive)

These need to feel objective — list multiple companies with honest
pros/cons. Soft-sell yourself. Otherwise it reads like an advert
and Google demotes.

## Prioritised build order

If we ship in tiers:

**Tier 1 (do first — 4 weeks of work):**
- 5 cost/pricing pages (Gap 3) — highest volume, anchors the cluster
- 20 corridor pages — top routes from GSC data (Gap 2)
- 50 service × town pages — top 10 towns × 5 services (Gap 1)

Subtotal: **~75 strategic new pages**, designed for high CTR + high commercial intent.

**Tier 2 (next 4-6 weeks):**
- 100 more service × town pages — next 20 towns
- 10 more corridor pages
- 3-5 urgency pages (Gap 4)

**Tier 3 (after Tier 1 results are in):**
- Comparison/best pages (Gap 5)
- Property-type pages (flat, studio, large house)
- Specialist niche pages (student, pensioner, business relocation)

## Risk warning

**Do not** generate all 3,968 possible service × town pages as
boilerplate. Google's "Helpful Content Update" actively demotes
sites with thin programmatically-generated pages. Each new page
needs:

- A genuinely unique angle (local detail, real pricing, specific
  service nuances for that town)
- 500-2,000 words of substantive content
- Town-specific imagery if possible
- Internal links to/from the existing town page so it's part of
  a topical cluster, not orphaned

A focused 200 high-quality pages beat 3,000 thin ones by 10x.

## Two things only you can decide

1. **Which tier first?** Cost/pricing pages have the highest
   organic traffic upside. Service × town pages have the highest
   conversion rate. Corridor pages sit in the middle. I'd default
   to cost pages → service × town → corridor.

2. **Which competitors are you willing to compare against?**
   Naming AnyVan, Compare My Move, Removals.com, Rubix Move etc.
   in comparison pages will rank you for their brand searches,
   but they may retaliate with their own pages. Worth it if you're
   confident on positioning.
