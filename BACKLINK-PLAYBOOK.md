## Section 2 - Estate agent partnerships

The pitch: **you offer their clients a moving discount, they get a
small commission and a "preferred mover" link on their site (which
backlinks you).** Both sides win.

### Why estate agents

- Their customers are about to move (perfect timing)
- They prefer recommending a vetted mover over leaving clients to find one (less stress for the agent)
- Each agency site has decent DA in their local area
- One partnership = 5-20 referrals/month if done right

### Who to target (in priority order)

1. **Independent local agents** in your highest-impression GSC towns:
   Rochester, Maidstone, Sevenoaks, Chelmsford, Grays, Chichester,
   Crawley. Independents have flexibility chains don't.
2. **Lettings agents** (faster turnover than sales = more frequent
   moves)
3. **New-build sales offices** for housing developments
4. **Online agents** (Purplebricks, Strike, Yopa) - longer shot, but
   one yes is worth 10 nos
5. **Probate solicitors** (handle inherited-property moves, low
   competition for this niche)

### How to find the right contact

- LinkedIn: search "[town] estate agent" or "lettings manager" - aim
  for branch managers or owners of independents
- The agency website itself usually has an "About" page with named
  staff; pick the most senior who isn't the receptionist
- Companies House for owner names of independents

### What "good" looks like

- **Target:** 20 personalised outreach emails in week 1 -> 5
  conversations -> 2-3 partnerships
- **Stretch:** 5 partnerships in month 1 = ~25-100 extra bookings/month
  by month 2-3
- **Backlink value:** even partnerships that produce zero bookings
  still give you a backlink

## Section 3 - Local press pitches

Press mentions = high-authority backlinks + brand recognition. The
trick is to **pitch a story, not your business**. Journalists need
content, not adverts.

### Outlets that matter for you

| Outlet | Region | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| KentOnline | Kent | Largest news site in Kent, DA 76 |
| KentLive | Kent | Sister to MyLondon. Strong local SEO. |
| Essex Live | Essex | Same group as KentLive. |
| BBC News London | London | Hardest to crack, biggest payoff. |
| The Times "Bricks & Mortar" | National property | Long shot, huge if landed |
| Estate Agent Today | Industry trade | Easier to land, links from EA sites |

### Angle 1 - The data angle (highest hit rate)

**Pitch:** "We moved 250 customers from London to Kent last year.
Here's the data on which London boroughs are emptying fastest, and
which Kent towns are filling up."

**Why it works:** Local journalists love data they can't get
elsewhere. Quote your own job log (anonymised). You become the
"removals industry source" they quote next year too.

**Email subject:** Story idea: Where Londoners are moving to in Kent
in 2026

**Body:**
```
Hi [journo name],

I run Relokates Removals - we handle about 30 moves a month from
London into Kent, so we sit on a year of route data nobody else
collates.

A few headlines from our job log:
- 60% of London-to-Kent moves go to Medway towns (Rochester,
  Chatham, Gillingham)
- Average move size has grown from 2-bed to 3-bed in 18 months
- Friday moves outnumber Saturday by 3:1 because of completion days

If a "where Londoners are moving" piece interests you, I can pull
together anonymised data + put you in touch with 2-3 recent customers
who'd be happy to share their move story.

Amos | Relokates Removals
07359 724844 - info@relokates.co.uk
```

### Angle 2 - The Dubai corridor angle

**Pitch:** "UK to Dubai removals tripled since 2024. Our customers
are middle-class professionals, not just bankers."

Targets: BBC News London, The Times, Telegraph (all run Britain-to-
Dubai stories regularly because the demographic is changing).

### Angle 3 - The cost-of-living angle

**Pitch:** "Average house move cost in [region] has dropped 12%
versus 2023 because of [reason]. Here's how to budget your move
properly."

Localises easily - separate pitches to KentOnline ("average Kent
move cost..."), Essex Live ("average Essex move cost..."). Different
data per region.

### Angle 4 - The behind-the-scenes angle

**Pitch:** "Why 30% of moves go wrong - a removal company breakdown."

Trade outlets love this. Sets you up as the honest, transparent
operator. Quote yourself: "the three things customers should ask
before booking any mover".

### Angle 5 - The seasonal angle (timeable for August)

**Pitch:** "End of school holidays = end of moving season. Why August
30th is the busiest day in our calendar."

Hyperlocal news picks up timely pieces. Easy to localise.

### Follow-up cadence

- Send pitch Tuesday morning (best open rate)
- One follow-up Friday if no reply
- Don't chase after two attempts; pitch a different angle next month
