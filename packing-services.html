Room-by-room packing guide

Different rooms require different packing approaches. Here is how our professional packers approach each area of your home:

Kitchen - the most challenging room

Plates packed vertically, never flat. Each plate wrapped individually in packing paper. Glasses packed upright with tissue inside. Cast iron and heavy cookware in small boxes only. All liquids double-wrapped and upright. Appliances in original boxes where possible or custom-wrapped.

Living room

TVs and monitors travel vertically in purpose-made TV boxes. Pictures and mirrors in corner protectors with multiple bubble wrap layers. Books in small boxes only - large boxes of books cannot be safely lifted. Ornaments individually wrapped with no void space in boxes.

Bedrooms

Wardrobe boxes for hanging clothes - no folding required. Mattress bags for all mattresses. Bed frames disassembled, screws in labelled bags taped to the frame. Bedding in large boxes or vacuum compression bags to reduce volume.

The golden rule - no void space

Every box is filled to the top with scrunched packing paper so items cannot move during transit. A well-packed box does not rattle. This single practice prevents more damage than any other packing technique.

The honest case for professional packing

Why most people underestimate the packing job

The average three-bedroom house contains approximately 400 to 600 individual items that need to be packed. Most people start packing two weeks before moving day, pack for a few hours each evening, and then face a chaotic final 48 hours trying to pack everything that is left. This leads to items being thrown into boxes without protection, boxes being overfilled or underfilled, and a significant proportion of fragile items arriving damaged.

A professional packing team with the right materials packs a three-bedroom house in 6 to 8 hours. Everything is protected, every box is correctly filled, every box is labelled. The cost of professional packing is almost always less than the cost of replacing one or two damaged items.
