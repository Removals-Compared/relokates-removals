Piano removals - our specialist process

Pianos are one of the most technically challenging items to move safely. The wrong approach causes irreparable damage to the instrument and serious risk of injury to the crew. Here is how our specialist piano team approaches every piano removal:

1
Pre-move assessmentWe assess the piano type, size, condition and access at both properties before quoting. Grand pianos, baby grands, upright pianos and concert grands all require different equipment and crew sizes.
2
Specialist equipment selectionPiano skates, specialist boards, heavy-duty strapping and protective covers are selected based on the specific instrument. Grand pianos require partial disassembly - legs and pedal lyre removed.
3
Protected transitThe piano is fully wrapped in protective covers, secured to the piano board and loaded with the correct orientation for transit. Grand pianos travel on their side on a specialist piano trolley.
4
Placement and reassemblyThe piano is placed exactly where you want it at the new property. Grand piano legs and pedal lyre are reassembled. We recommend a tuning appointment 2 to 4 weeks after the move once the instrument has acclimatised.

Fine art and gallery moves

Relokates works with private collectors, galleries, auction houses and art dealers across London, Essex, Kent and West Sussex. Our fine art service includes:

Acid-free packing materials

All artwork is packed using acid-free tissue, pH-neutral foam and specialist art blankets. No materials that could cause chemical damage to canvas, frame or gilding.

Bespoke crating

Large or fragile works requiring maximum protection are crated individually. Crates are constructed to the exact dimensions of the piece with internal cushioning and secure fastening.

Chain of custody documentation

For gallery and dealer moves, we provide full condition reports at collection and delivery with photographic documentation. Essential for insurance and provenance records.

Climate awareness during transit

Extreme temperatures and humidity changes damage canvas, wood frames and sensitive media. We time fine art moves to avoid temperature extremes and use appropriate vehicle conditions.

Specialist insurance for high-value items

🛡
Important - standard insurance has per-item limits

Our standard goods-in-transit insurance covers items up to a per-item value. For fine art, antiques, jewellery and other high-value items that exceed this threshold, we strongly recommend arranging specialist declared value insurance. We can advise on the options available and the documentation required.

Always declare high-value items when requesting your luxury removal quote.
