UAE customs - what you need to know

UAE customs requirements are strict and specific. Understanding them in advance avoids delays and complications at the Dubai end:

Residency visa requirement

UAE customs requires evidence of UAE residency to import household goods duty-free. Your residency visa or Emirates ID must be in place before your shipment arrives in Dubai.

Prohibited items

UAE prohibited items include alcohol, pork products, narcotics, weapons, certain medications, gambling equipment and politically sensitive material. We provide a full prohibited items list during your consultation.

Packing inventory requirement

UAE customs requires a detailed packing inventory of every item in your shipment with declared values. Our export packing team produces this documentation as part of the packing process.

Electronics and high-value items

Electronics, jewellery and high-value items require individual declaration with purchase receipts or valuation certificates. Undeclared high-value items can be seized by UAE customs.

Why Dubai

The UK to Dubai corridor - what you need to know

Thousands of British families, professionals and businesses relocate to Dubai every year. Here is what drives the demand and what makes this route specialist.

Who relocates from the UK to Dubai

The UK to Dubai route is dominated by three groups. Finance and banking professionals relocating for roles in Dubai International Financial Centre. Technology and construction professionals moving for project-based contracts. And British families seeking a lifestyle change - lower tax environment, year-round sunshine, high quality international schools and a large British expat community.

Dubai's residential areas most popular with British expats include Dubai Marina, Jumeirah, Arabian Ranches, Emirates Hills, Mirdif and the Palm Jumeirah. Each area has different property types - apartments in Dubai Marina, villas in Arabian Ranches and Emirates Hills - which affects container size requirements and delivery access.

Our experience on this specific route means we understand not just the logistics but the practicalities - which Dubai areas have difficult vehicle access, which community management companies require advance notice, and what the realistic timeline looks like from the day you confirm your move to the day your belongings arrive at your Dubai address.

Planning your international move

Start planning your Dubai removal at least 12 weeks out

International removals have a longer lead time than domestic moves. UAE customs documentation, shipping booking, export packing and residency visa requirements all need to be in place before your belongings can depart the UK. Starting the process at least 12 weeks before your planned move date gives adequate time for all of these elements to come together without rushing.

The most common mistake we see is clients contacting us 4 to 6 weeks before their move date expecting sea freight delivery in Dubai within their timeline. Sea freight alone is 5 to 9 weeks. Add export packing, shipping booking and UAE customs clearance and the minimum realistic timeline from first call to Dubai delivery is 10 to 14 weeks for sea freight.

If you are relocating to Dubai, call us as early as possible - even before you have a confirmed move date. An early consultation costs nothing and gives you a realistic picture of the timeline and costs involved.
